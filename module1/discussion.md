# Module 1 Discussion: First Impressions

This is the discussion thread for Module 1. It opens with this module and stays open through the rest of the term. You will post an initial response here after working through the readings and the four listening guide tracks, and you are welcome to return to the thread later in the course as your thinking develops.

## The prompt

Pick one of the four Module 1 anchor tracks (Sam Cooke, Celia Cruz with the Fania All-Stars, Sugar Pie DeSanto, or Hank Williams) and write about your strongest reaction to it. Be specific: name at least one moment in the recording (a particular line, a production choice, a vocal gesture, a textural shift) and say what that moment did to you and why.

If it helps, think about why you reacted the way you did and what you bring to listening as a person. The framing reading asked you to consider your own starting point. This is an invitation to put that thinking into words. You do not have to disclose anything personal you do not want to share. Notice when a track pulls you in, notice when one pushes you away, and ask yourself why with whatever honesty feels right.

## What a strong post looks like

A strong post does three things:

- It picks a specific track and a specific moment within it. Not "the song made me feel sad" but "the way Cooke's voice cracks on 'I'm afraid to die' made me feel something I am still trying to put into words."
- It moves from describing what is in the music to claiming something about what the music is doing. The methodology reading called this the move from description to argument.
- It is yours. It does not summarize the listening guide or the artist's biography. It tells us what you heard and what you think about it.

Two to four paragraphs is the right length. Longer is fine if you have more to say. Shorter is fine if you say it well.

## Responding to classmates

After your initial post, respond to at least two classmates' posts. Good responses do one or more of these things:

- Add to a point a classmate made by pointing at another moment in the same track or a different one.
- Disagree thoughtfully. Different listeners hear different things. Saying "I had a different reaction, here is why" is welcome and useful.
- Ask a question that takes the conversation further. Not "great post!" but "what do you think the bridge is doing in that song?"

You will be coming back to this thread across the term. Treat it like a conversation that has time, not a one-shot assignment.
