# MUS 302: What to Listen for in Music

Course materials for MUS 302, an online asynchronous upper-division
general education course at California State University, East Bay,
taught by Inés Thiebaut.

The course surveys American popular music from the 1940s forward,
organized around four cultural traditions and the dialogues between
them: African American foundational traditions, Latin diasporic
traditions, Asian American traditions, and European American
immigrant and working-class traditions.

This repository hosts the interactive listening guides students
work through during the term. Each guide is a single HTML page
with embedded audio, glossed vocabulary, and contextual photos.
